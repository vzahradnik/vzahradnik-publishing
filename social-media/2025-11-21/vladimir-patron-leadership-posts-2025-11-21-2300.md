# **1. VLADIMÍR – Facebook Variant (warm, practical, personal)**

In business, most people are paid strictly for output. Every hour gets measured. Every minute is directed toward KPIs.

Recently, I decided to try something different.

A friend of mine has been exploring automation tools like n8n. Instead of treating it as a side distraction, I funded his time — even when I didn’t have enough work for him.

I pay him to study, to experiment, to grow.

Yes, he might leave one day. Yes, we don’t have a contract that binds him to me. But I still believe it’s the right approach.

If I manage to secure more funding, a meaningful part will always go to self-study. No strings attached.

Because people grow the most when they feel trusted — not monitored.

# **2. VLADIMÍR – LinkedIn Variant (professional, concise, leadership-focused)**

Most companies pay for output. They track every hour and optimize for KPIs.

I decided to take a different approach.

A friend and part-time collaborator recently started exploring automation. Instead of limiting him to tasks, I funded his learning time — even during periods when I didn’t have enough work for him.

I pay him to study, to experiment, and to build skills.

Could he leave? Yes. We don’t have a contract. But investing in people is still the right strategy.

If I secure additional funding, I’ll continue allocating a portion toward self-study and skill development, no strings attached.

Long-term growth always starts with trust.
